# External Review And Reproduction Certificates Theorem

## Target

Construct a finite, network-native external review and reproduction
certificate interface downstream of the closed Paper 15 prediction and
falsification protocols theorem.

The target is a conditional theorem only. It should eventually specify how
review records, reproduction records, artifact hashes, environment
descriptors, and audit statuses can be stated as finite certificate rows
without silently turning certificate definition into review acceptance,
reproduction success, physical validation, or unified-field promotion.

## Closed Rungs

- `ERRC-001`: Upstream binding and claim-boundary scaffold through the frozen
  Paper 15 final conditional certificate.
- `ERRC-002`: Finite external review and reproduction certificate records.
- `ERRC-003`: Finite reviewer, protocol, and provenance descriptors.
- `ERRC-004`: Finite reproduction artifact, environment, and hash descriptors.
- `ERRC-005`: Paper 15 protocol compatibility without review acceptance or
  reproduction success.
- `ERRC-006`: Certificate stability, auditability, and reproducibility behavior
  as finite re-checkability only.

## Active Rung

- `ERRC-007`: Audit hidden promotion, validation, review-acceptance,
  reproduction-success, and adjacent success imports.

Required boundary for `ERRC-007`:

- no certificate recovery claim
- no review acceptance claim
- no reproduction success claim
- no benchmark, prediction, or falsification success claim
- no physical promotion
- no physical validation
- no empirical adequacy claim
- no observed-catalog recovery claim
- no simulation-only or fit-only shortcut
- no unified field theory claim

## Planned Rungs

- `ERRC-008`: final conditional certificate

## Residual Risk

The main risk is category error: confusing a review/reproduction certificate
interface with evidence that review accepted the program or reproduction
succeeded. Guards must keep certificate construction separate from external
acceptance, reproduction success, physical validation, and physical promotion.
